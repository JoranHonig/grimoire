Agents are actually pretty good.

Too many skills, too much architecture, too much fluff. It doesn't actually help. It gets in the way.

Every grimoire agent and skill is there to support a specific workflow, to provide specific guidance in som cases, but is oriented towards letting the model shine.

### Exploration over Stored Summaries

It is tempting to have agents build a complex knowledge base with lots of information and documentation on the project with the idea that this mimics how humans think and learn.

In practice it's better to have fresh contexts and have agents re-explore and rediscover the codebase. This allows them to build context in relation to the current task / hypothesis.
